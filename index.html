About The Project

What is Friction?

Friction is the force that resists motion between surfaces. It can be both beneficial and problematic depending on the situation.

Why Necessary?

Without friction we couldn't walk, drive, write, or control movement. It enables countless daily activities.

Why Evil?

Friction causes wear, heat generation, energy loss, and reduced efficiency in machines.

Key Insights

Energy Loss

Mechanical systems lose energy because of friction.

Heat Generation

Friction converts kinetic energy into thermal energy.

Control & Safety

Vehicles rely on friction for braking and stability.

Industrial Impact

Engineers reduce friction using lubricants and smart materials.
